## 1. EQUIPMENT ESSENTIALS

### Minimum Requirements (Budget Setup)
- **Smartphone**: iPhone 11+ or Android 2020+
- **Tripod**: $20-30 smartphone tripod
- **Light Source**: Window light or ring light ($30)
- **Background**: White poster board or wall

### Recommended Setup (Professional)
- **Camera**: DSLR/Mirrorless with 50mm lens
- **Tripod**: Sturdy camera tripod ($50-100)
- **Lighting**: Softbox lights or ring light ($100-200)
- **Background**: Professional backdrop system ($50-100)

### Advanced Setup (Studio Quality)
- **Camera**: Full-frame camera with prime lenses
- **Lighting**: 3-point lighting system
- **Background**: Seamless paper backdrops
- **Software**: Adobe Lightroom/Photoshop

## 2. LIGHTING SETUP

### Natural Light (Best Option)
**Setup:**
- Position subject near north-facing window
- Shoot 2-3 hours after sunrise or before sunset
- Use white foam board to bounce light onto shadows

**Tips:**
- Avoid direct sunlight (causes harsh shadows)
- Overcast days = perfect diffused lighting
- Golden hour = warm, flattering light

### Artificial Light (When Natural Isn't Available)

**Ring Light Setup:**
- Place ring light 2-3 feet from subject
- Position at 45-degree angle
- Adjust brightness to avoid overexposure

**Softbox Setup:**
- Main light: 45-degree angle to subject
- Fill light: Opposite side, 50% brightness
- Back light: Behind subject, for separation

## 3. COMPOSITION TECHNIQUES

### Rule of Thirds
- Divide frame into 3x3 grid
- Place subject on intersection points
- More dynamic than centered shots

### Leading Lines
- Use lines to draw eye to subject
- Examples: desk edges, monitor edges, keyboard

### Framing
- Use elements to frame your subject
- Examples: doorway, window, monitor

### Symmetry & Patterns
- Perfect for service demonstrations
- Shows organization and professionalism

## 5. EDITING & RETOUCHING

### Basic Edits (Essential)
1. **Straighten**: Align horizons
2. **Crop**: Remove distractions
3. **Exposure**: Adjust brightness
4. **Contrast**: Add depth
5. **Color**: Correct white balance

### Advanced Edits (Optional)
1. **Remove blemishes**: Clean up background
2. **Sharpen**: Enhance details
3. **Vignette**: Focus attention
4. **Color grading**: Set mood

### Recommended Apps
- **Free**: Snapseed, VSCO, Lightroom Mobile
- **Paid**: Lightroom, Photoshop, Capture One

## 8. QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE

### 5-Minute Setup Checklist
- [ ] Clean workspace
- [ ] Set up tripod
- [ ] Position lighting
- [ ] Check background
- [ ] Test shot
- [ ] Adjust settings
- [ ] Take multiple shots
- [ ] Review and select best

### Camera Settings (Smartphone)
- **Mode**: Portrait or Pro
- **Grid**: On (for composition)
- **HDR**: On (for balanced lighting)
- **Resolution**: Highest available
- **Focus**: Tap on subject

### Camera Settings (DSLR)
- **Mode**: Aperture Priority (A/Av)
- **Aperture**: f/2.8 - f/5.6
- **ISO**: 100-400 (low light: 800)
- **White Balance**: Custom or Auto
- **File Format**: RAW + JPEG

### Editing Workflow
1. **Import** and organize photos
2. **Select** best shots
3. **Crop** and straighten
4. **Adjust** exposure and contrast
5. **Correct** color and white balance
6. **Remove** distractions
7. **Sharpen** appropriately
8. **Export** for web (sRGB, 72 DPI)

### File Naming Convention
`YYYY-MM-DD_Service_Description_Version.jpg`

Examples:
- `2024-01-15_Web_Design_Homepage_V1.jpg`
- `2024-01-15_Logo_Design_Final_V2.jpg`

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Photos are too dark
- Move closer to light source
- Increase exposure compensation
- Use reflector to bounce light

### Photos have harsh shadows
- Diffuse light source
- Add fill light
- Move further from window

### Colors look wrong
- Adjust white balance
- Shoot in RAW format
- Edit color temperature

### Photos look blurry
- Use tripod
- Increase shutter speed
- Clean camera lens
